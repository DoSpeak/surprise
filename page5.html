Dear Akshu,
There are some people you meet and you just know. Not immediately — that's a movie thing. In real life it's quieter than that. It's more like one day you're going about your life perfectly fine, and then somewhere between one conversation and the next, the bar just moves. And suddenly “fine” doesn't feel like enough anymore because you've seen what it looks like when it's actually good. That's what you did. You ruined the average, Akshu. Completely. And I'm not even mad about it.
Same school, actually. Different sections — ICSE and CBSE living their completely separate lives — which meant we never once crossed paths in all those years. Maybe that's just how it was meant to go. Some people meet at the right time, not the first available one. Then college happened, we actually met, and we both, independently, silently decided the other one was just a little too much. I distinctly remember thinking you were intense. You almost certainly thought something equally unflattering about me. And honestly? Were we wrong? Were we right? Does it even matter now? And then — the library. Some random afternoon. Some accidental, completely unplanned conversation. And something just quietly flipped. I can't tell you exactly what was said or how long it lasted. I just know that by the end of it, something had changed. The person I'd mentally filed under “too much” had somehow become the person I wanted to talk to again tomorrow.
Everything after that just kept adding up. The tea stalls — always the tea stalls, always somehow the best conversations happening over the worst plastic cups. The food, which you speak about with a level of passion and specificity that I genuinely respect and find slightly unhinged. The long evening walks where we went from laughing about nothing to accidentally solving our lives without meaning to. The advice — real advice, the kind that stings a little because it's accurate and you know it is. The reality checks we hand each other with zero ceremony. The sarcasm we throw so freely that anyone else would think we're fighting. We know we're not. And then underneath all of it — this quiet, completely unspoken, never-directly-acknowledged care that we both do for each other without ever making it a big deal. That part. That's the part that means the most.
My sunshine. My sugarplum. My honey bunch. These are not random things I call you — they are genuinely what you feel like to be around. You are warm and bright and present in a way that makes any room feel different when you're in it. You are also, simultaneously, one of the most wonderfully contradictory people I've ever met. Studious and completely chaotic. Wise enough to say things I'll think about for days, and also carrying this beautiful, unguarded simplicity in the smallest moments — the kind I don't just smile at, I genuinely love it. You play cricket like you have something to prove. You talk about food the way poets talk about the sea — like it has layers, like it deserves that kind of attention, and honestly, it does when you describe it. You will talk for hours — and I will listen, genuinely, every single time — and then you will fall completely asleep the moment the room goes quiet. You have a happy heart and a thoughtful soul and an absolutely fighting spirit that I have been on the receiving end of and frankly have no complaints. All of it is you. All of it is what makes being your friend feel like something genuinely rare.
Twenty is a strange number to land on. It sounds more grown-up than it feels — like a word that belongs to someone more certain, more sorted, more put-together than either of us currently are on any given Tuesday. But here's what I keep thinking: all the versions of you that came before this moment don't disappear. They just stay. The loud one and the quiet one. The one who shows up without being asked and the one who somehow knows exactly what to say. The one whose presence alone makes things feel okay. None of that goes anywhere. It just gets more. I've watched your chapters happen close enough to know that each one has been better than the last — and I have absolutely no reason to think that stops now.
I'm not good at endings — you know this, you've watched me try — so I'll just say it plainly. I am so genuinely, inexpressibly happy that you are in my life. That the library happened. That we both somehow got over ourselves long enough to let it. Here's to twenty. Here's to every tea stall and every walk and every ridiculous argument and every real conversation still ahead of us. Nothing is going to ruin this. It's only going to keep growing. This one is for life, Akshu. I mean it completely. No take-backs.
with all the love I have —
Kartik
P.S. — The back injury. I put it in the letter so you actually have to acknowledge it. Take care of yourself. I'm watching.
