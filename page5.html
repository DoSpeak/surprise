Dear Akshu,
I have been thinking about what to say to you for a long time. Not because I don't have words — but because there are too many of them, and none of them feel quite big enough for what I actually want to say. So let me try anyway.
I remember the first time I realised this friendship was something different. Something that wasn't going anywhere. Something that was going to last. You are the kind of person who comes into your life and quietly rearranges everything — not loudly, not dramatically, just steadily, until one day you realise you can't imagine it without them.
You are the most sensible person I know — and also the most chaotic. You are the most mature — and also the biggest sleepyhead. You are fiercely studious and somehow still the one who makes everything more fun. You play cricket like you mean it, you talk about food like it's poetry, and you talk in general like every single word is worth listening to — and it is, Akshu. I could listen to you forever.
My chubby cheek. My sugarplum. My honey bunch. My sunshine. These aren't just nicknames — they are what you actually feel like. Warm and sweet and bright and completely yours. You are comfortable and loud and quiet and silly and deep all at once. And once you let someone in — there is nothing like it. I am so lucky you decided I was yours.
Today you are turning 20. And I want you to know that every single year that made you who you are — the baby with the biggest smile in the room, the girl in the lehenga twirling under the lights, the one watching sunsets from boats, the one singing songs and dancing like no one is watching — every one of those years matters. Every single one of those years is you.
Here's to this chapter. Here's to the ones that follow. Here's to every laugh we haven't laughed yet and every memory we haven't made yet. This bond is for life, Akshu. I mean that completely.
with all the love I have —
Kartik
